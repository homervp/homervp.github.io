Film Making

During my film making endaevors and dives into behind the scenes, making ofs and interviews, I have collected a list of solid resources and tools for film makers to use and learn from. Below you will find links to YouTube channels, tools, websites and books that I have found consistently useful and interesting.

Tutorial channels

Corridor Crew

The team of professional VFX artists at Corridor Digital make the long-running series "VFX artists react", where they break down scenes from movies and TV and how they were made. They often feature guests from the industry for additional insight and insider stories. Their breakdowns are an excellent way to discover new techniques while the videos are also fun to watch. Smaller series they work on are "Stuntment react" and "Animators react", while they often produce VFX-centered videos for fun, and occasionally film their own internal competitions, where team members produce a scene on their own and later gather to watch it.

Captain Disilusion

The Captain takes it upon himself to debunk viral videos and other forms of visual trickery, usually reproducing the effects in the process.

Erik Does VFX

Erik recreates scenes from holywood movies from scratch, typically in one week. This is a great example of how creativity and modern tools allow someone to achieve highly ambitious scenes.

Ian Hubert

Ian Hubert made a name for himself through the "Lazy Tutorials" series. While most YouTube tutorials on VFX, animation and similar subjects tend to take over 20 minutes to show a basic function, Ian's tutorials have consistently stuck to 1 minute in duration, while still demonstrating impressive results and very smart use of techniques.

Indy Mogul

I first found this channel through its Backyard FX series, which has unfortunately been discontinued but has left us a lot of material. However they also covered all sorts of technical aspects, such as lighting, equipment and production.

Rocket Jump Film School

Writing

Overly Sarcastic Productions

OSP is a duo that talks about storytelling, mythology and history. Most relevant are two series, in the Trope Talk sries OSP Red does a deep dive into one trope per episode, discussing its history and how it is used, strenghts and weaknesses and lesser known applications. In the Detailed Diatribe, the two hosts do 1-2 hour long deep dives into specific entries or franchises. OSP is one of those channels I check weekly for new uploads, always interesting and funny and very informative.

The Second Story

The runner of this channel walks through some very frequently covered topics, covering them with impressive density and detail, while also reviewing commo advice and explaining, with examples, why things can work or backfire. In the very first video I watched, multiple things clicked in a new way, and I learned some new storywriting techniques that I hadn't heard of before.

Terrible Writing Advice

Each episode grabs one theme, trope, genre etc, and goes through all the common mistakes, giving you a solid idea of what writing it poorly looks like. Do the opposite, and it should turn out fine.

Just Write

This channel is most notable for its extensive collection of interviews with high-profile figures in the film industry, ranging from actors to directors to writers. The interviews are fairly focused, while on occasion compilations of over two hours with multiple artists are featured.

Free* Resources

The websites of this list provide a lot of free assets, but not all assets are free.

Additionally, I try to verify that the licenses are CC0 or CC-Attribution, i.e. can be used without sacrificing your own IP and copyright (as in Share-alike) or potential for monetization (non-commercial). BUT this does not mean this is universally true, some are free for artists and studios making under X amount per year.

Finally, some websites may ask for "peculiar" information, such as a full name and address to make out the license to, or a phone number. I try to note these peculiarities but keep it in mind.

ActionVFX

https://www.actionvfx.com/

Peculiarity: Needs address for licensing

License: Subset of assets is free for studios or individuals with revenue under threshold

Provides ~28 collections of commonly useful 2D, 3D and audio assets, such as surface imperfections, smoke, portals and fire effects for free. The free versions of the effects are in 2k resolution, while 4k resolution is available in the pro subscription

Make Big Films

https://www.makebigfilms.com/collections/free%20effects

Peculiarity: None

License: NOT CHECKED

Collection of action VFX (explosions, smoke, muzzle flashes etc) provided for free.

MixKit

Peculiarity: NOT CHECKED

License: NOT CHECKED
