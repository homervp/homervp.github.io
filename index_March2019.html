About me

Who am I

I'm Homer, a computer engineering graduate, Space Engineering postgraduate student, software and game developper by hobby. I'm especially talented at software architecture and design, and most thoroughly enjoy creating configurable, scriptable software and class libraries that allows for diverse execution scenarios without modifying the underlying executables.

In my free time I play boardgames, videogames, keyboards, and occasionally I delve into creative and artistic activities, including sketching, scale modelling and CGI.

Studies

Academically, I am currently in the Space Engineering MSc program on the University of Surrey, and I've just graduated from the Computer Engineering & Informatics program of the University of Patras polytechnic school (5 years, 300ECTS, Integrated MSc in Greece as of 2018, NARIC still lists these programs as BSc(Hons)). During my MSc I will be working on a thesis in astrodynamics, and am interested in pursuing a PhD in space engineering or astrophysics, as I have developped an increasing interest in the physics of the cosmos.

As far as what exactly I actually and practically do, I'm a software engineer by training and have some contact with systems engineering through my internship at the European Space Agency, all the while I am familiarizing with simulation methods for various physical phenomena. In my free time I mainly develop desktop applications for the Windows in C#, and occasionally video games. In the past, I have been a member of the Microsoft Student Partners for 5 years, serving through the Patras chapter of the Student Guru student society through which MSPs in Greece organize their events. There I presented on average 4 live programming walkthroghs per year. From February 2018 to September 2019 I served as Community Manager for the Student Guru and Microsoft Student Partner community in Patras, and fulfulled Region Manager duties for the UPatras-AUEB-OpenUniversity communities. During this time I reorganized the community, re-establishing our online organization methods. I have been a member of the UPatras Robotics Club for a year during which I mostly helped with minor performance improvements in the maze solver's algorithm. In 2019 I joined the University of Surrey Model UN society, and attended ULMUN at King's College as delegate of the D.R.Congo in the HRC commitee on IDPs, where we reached an agreement signed by all the delegates except one which abstained.

Work experience

As mentioned I have completed a six-month (July 2017 - December 2017) paid traineeship in the European Space Operations Cener (ESOC) of the European Space Agency (ESA) in Frankfurt, where I worked on Systems Engineering. Specifically I was developping a shadow-engineering tool that transfered structured information from the Euclid Ground Segment's database into Enterprise Architect and proceded to generate charts for each element, as well as a couple of design documents to near indistinguishable similarity with the originals.

After I returned from Germany, I did a three-month paid university internship at the National Healthcare Services ambulance depot near the university. I had already volunteered there for maintenance, support and upgrade of their systems for over a year, when they requested assistance from a professor, who in turn asked if we were interested. After over a year of assisting as volunteers, he suggested to register it as an internship.
